<figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1000/1*JTujwEZUGzsJHpb1fV97Yg.png" /></figure><p>In our <strong>March Developer Webinar</strong>, we moved past the usual ‘what’s new’ and tackled the ‘what’s next’. From the rise of AI-driven ‘vibe coding’ to the rollout of granular scopes, the ecosystem is evolving fast.</p><p>If you couldn’t make it, you can <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3FLcFsdFn_w"><strong>watch the full recording on YouTube.</strong></a></p><a href="https://medium.com/media/86d84d5501f5e9722b6ab6f5495ac480/href">https://medium.com/media/86d84d5501f5e9722b6ab6f5495ac480/href</a><p><strong>Meet the panel</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Corey Leung: </strong>Developer Marketing Manager (Host)</li><li><strong>Regan Ashworth:</strong> Head of Ecosystem Governance</li><li><strong>Breana McHugh:</strong> Senior API Technical Specialist</li><li><strong>Parikshit Ghosh: </strong>Senior Product Manager</li><li><strong>Amanda Moyle:</strong> Content &amp; Merchandising Manager</li><li><strong>Millie Soutter: </strong>Senior Developer Marketing Manager</li><li><strong>Annie Terry: </strong>Head of Platform Marketing</li></ul><h3>The fireside chat: Is everyone a developer now?</h3><p>The highlight of our session was a conversation between our host, Corey Leung, and Regan Ashworth, Head of Ecosystem Governance, about the ‘vibe coding’ movement.</p><p>We’re seeing a fundamental shift where describing a problem in natural language is becoming a valid way to build software. Using AI-native tools like Replit, Lovable, or Claude Code, builders can create applications at the speed of thought.</p><p><strong>What is vibe coding?</strong> Regan likened it to the transition from manual to automatic transmissions in cars. Vibe coding lowers the barrier to entry, allowing non-technical founders and designers to prototype and deploy apps in record time simply by describing what they want to achieve.</p><p><strong>The engineer’s role as a ‘force multiplier’</strong> While the barrier is lower, the role of the professional engineer has never been more important. Proficiency in traditional engineering remains a massive advantage; experienced developers can use these tools to manage multiple sub-agents simultaneously. This turns AI into a force multiplier, allowing you to focus on high-level architecture rather than getting bogged down in syntax.</p><p><strong>The quality bar remains</strong> While anyone can now prototype an app, Xero still maintains a strict quality bar for the Xero App Store. Security, hosting, maintenance, and long-term support are still the domain of the professional engineer. We’re excited to see how you use these tools to build more robust, innovative solutions for the Xero community.</p><h3>Technical deep dive: Scopes and webhooks</h3><p>We’re shipping updates that make your integrations more secure and your development environment more flexible.</p><p><strong>Granular scopes are here</strong></p><p>To give users more transparency, we’ve broken down our broad scopes into specific permissions.</p><p><strong>Accounting.transactions</strong> is now split into specific scopes like <strong>accounting.invoices</strong>, <strong>accounting.banktransactions,</strong> and <strong>accounting.manualjournals.</strong></p><ul><li><strong>Accounting.reports</strong> now offers individual scopes for every report.</li><li><strong>Why it matters:</strong> You can now request access to just the Profit and Loss report rather than the entire accounting suite.</li><li><strong>Timeline</strong>: These are already live for new apps created after<strong> 2 March 2026</strong>. Existing apps will see these added on<strong> 8 April</strong>, with a full migration deadline of <strong>27 September 2027.</strong></li></ul><h3>Credit Notes webhooks</h3><p>Our internal eventing platform, Horizon, is now generally available for external partners.</p><ul><li>Early adopters have seen nearly a <strong>50% drop in GET </strong>calls by switching from polling to these notifications.</li><li>You can activate these today via the Developer Portal &gt; webhooks tab &gt; subscribe to CreditNotes and then use the ‘Send test’ tool to verify the end point.</li></ul><h3>New toolkit for agentic workflows</h3><p>To help you build the next generation of AI-integrated apps, we’ve launched several new resources:</p><ol><li><strong>Local MCP server:</strong> A Model Context Protocol server that allows AI models like Claude or Cursor to interact directly with Xero APIs.</li><li><strong>Agentic SDKs:</strong> Reference implementations for LangChain, Google Agent Development Kit, and OpenAI.</li><li><strong>Developer CLI (alpha):</strong> A new command-line interface in developer preview to support frameworks that prefer terminal-based interaction.</li></ol><p>Check out <a href="https://developer.xero.com/ai"><strong>Xero’s AI Toolkit.</strong></a></p><figure><img alt="" src="https://cdn-images-1.medium.com/max/1024/1*g21IOf7HfDInqWGtMBIrXA.png" /></figure><h3>Xero Developer events and updates</h3><ul><li><strong>The Xero Developer growth program </strong>is always open for applications!
